Understanding the Surge in Out-of-State Buyers to Phoenix

The Phoenix housing market is a magnet for out-of-state buyers, fueled by factors like affordability, job opportunities, and lifestyle appeal. According to the National Association of Realtors (NAR) 2023 Profile of Home Buyers and Sellers, 13% of all U.S. home buyers were from out-of-state, up from 11% in 2022. In Arizona, this trend is even more pronounced, with United Van Lines' 2023 National Movers Study reporting a 58% inbound migration rate, ranking the state among the top 10 for net inbound moves.

Redfin's 2023 migration data highlights that 26% of home searches in the Phoenix metro area come from out-of-state users, primarily from Los Angeles (8%), Seattle (5%), and New York (4%). These buyers are often escaping high-cost coastal areas, where median home prices exceed $800,000 in California, compared to Phoenix's average of $450,000 per the Arizona Regional Multiple Listing Service (ARMLS) in 2023.

A Zillow report from 2023 found that out-of-state buyers accounted for 22% of home purchases in Arizona, above the national average of 18%. Virtual tours influence 35% of these decisions by enabling remote viewing, making them a game-changer for agents targeting this demographic.

Why Phoenix Appeals to Remote Buyers

Phoenix's allure lies in its blend of urban amenities and outdoor lifestyle. The Greater Phoenix Economic Council reported in 2023 that the metro area attracted over 50,000 net migrants, with 40% from out-of-state, driven by tech and healthcare job growth adding 70,000 positions per Bureau of Labor Statistics data.

Top feeder states include California (28% of inbound migrants), Texas (12%), and Washington (9%), according to a 2023 Arizona State University study. These buyers, often millennials and young professionals aged 25-44 (35% of new residents per U.S. Census Bureau 2023 data), are drawn to suburbs like Scottsdale and Chandler for family-friendly vibes and proximity to hiking trails.

The Power of Virtual Tours in Real Estate

Virtual tours have exploded in popularity, with Matterport's 2023 industry report showing a 40% increase in usage for real estate listings. Out-of-state buyers spend 2-3 times longer on properties with 3D tours compared to those with static photos, leading to faster decisions amid high interest rates.

In Phoenix, 60% of ARMLS listings in 2023 included virtual tours, up from 45% in 2022, helping homes sell in an average of 45 days. Platforms like Zillow and Realtor.com reported a 28% growth in VR tour views, correlating with a 20% higher likelihood of offers from remote buyers.

As NAR Chief Economist Lawrence Yun noted in a 2023 webinar, "Virtual tours are essential for out-of-state buyers, reducing the need for physical visits by 50% and enabling faster closings in markets like Phoenix where migration is high."

  • Immersive Experience: 3D tours allow buyers to explore every room, giving a true sense of space without travel.
  • Time Savings: Remote viewers can "walk through" multiple properties quickly, narrowing options efficiently.

  • Trust Building: High-quality visuals reduce uncertainty, leading to more competitive offers.
